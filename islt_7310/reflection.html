Personal Reflection

Academic and Career Background

I started this program one year after completing my bachelor's degree in Education from Towson University. Based on my academic interest with my undergraduate course in instructional technology and my camp counselor experience at a technology camp, I decided to expand my knowledge of learning technology by pursuing a master's degree at the University of Missouri, Columbia. During my attendance with this asynchronous program, I have actively engaged in online discussions and group projects with other peers to analyze the learning technology practices based on our articles and textbook reading. Eventually, I have demonstrated my understanding of instructional design through the ISD projects that I created from this program during my job interview. At about halfway through the program, I started my first position as an instructional designer. While balancing a full-time job and graduate school work at the same time was challenging, I was able to improve my time management and organizational skills.

Course Program Experience

I have gained a lot of practical skillsets and expertise in learning theories by taking project-based and interactive courses through hands-on activities from this program. Most of my coursework in this program involved demonstrating my instructional design skills through real-world projects. The skillsets that I developed from this program enabled me to design and develop interactive learning experiences supported by learning technology practices. For example, my interaction design project was based on the real-life context of creating a resource website to provide disability rights information to the employees of the existing organization called Disability Justice Coalition (DJC) within the Seattle Public School (SPS) union. Because it was a project based on real-life context, the design process of developing the prototype website was more engaging and relevant than creating a project based on a hypothetical scenario.

Improvements

Aside from gaining knowledge of the latest learning technology practices, my organizational skills with simultaneously handling multiple projects and tasks improved. I developed project management strategies to carry out projects with long-term durations. The professors have been supportive of my learning process through any guidance I needed for my academic success. I now have a much better structure for project-based routines.

Career Aspiration

As an instructional designer, I am excited to apply learning technology practices from this program in real life. I studied education for my undergraduate degree. I was familiar with writing learning objectives and designing learning activities based on my learning objectives as an education major. However, I lacked an understanding of the design process steps. As an instructional designer, I learned that I could conduct a needs assessment to identify the learning and training gaps of the target learners. I also learned how to design and develop learning content using multimedia and rapid development tools. Now, I am ready to apply my skills as an instructional designer to continue designing and developing eLearning modules. While my short- term goal is to gain more experience in developing eLearning courses as an instructional designer, my long-term goal is to incorporate my understanding of user experience practices to transition into a learning experience designer.
