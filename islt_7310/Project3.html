Goal 3: Leadership & Social Responsibility

Competencies: I developed competencies for socially responsible technology leadership.
Goal 3: To provide students with experiences to become socially responsible, reflective/sensitive on the interaction of technologies & society and act ethically in response to current and future challenges of emerging technologies for learning.

Artifact: Interaction Design - Disability Justice Coalition Website

Objective 3.1: Students demonstrate technology leadership and knowledge of ethics as applied to current and future socio-technical context.


Disability Justice Coalition (DJC) Website project was designed to address the need to decentralize information about disability rights to members of DJC organizations by creating a prototype website using Adobe XD. DJC is an organization within the Seattle Public Schools (SPS) union. The target audiences of this artifact are DJC members who need a website that would provide them with information about the disability rights of employees.
This assignment was a group project with another student. I have demonstrated leadership skills by leading video conferencing sessions to distribute our tasks and communicate the project status. This project involved an iterative process of building, testing, and revising based on our user testing result. Throughout the project, I constantly communicated how to improve our prototype design to look aesthetically more appealing and user-friendly based on the heuristic principles with my team member.
One strength of this artifact is the thorough process of conducting problem analysis, evaluation reports, low and high-fidelity prototypes, and usability testing to create a website prototype that addresses the need of my target audience. This artifact demonstrates the socio-technical context behind the interaction design problem and the types of heuristic principles to develop this website. I think my artifact shows strength in guiding through the overall design process and design improvements based on our user testing. One weakness of this artifact is the small number of users who participated in the usability testing. Due to time constraints, my team member and I did not have enough time to conduct the usability testing to a larger pool of participants other than the minimum requirement of 3 participants. If I were to work on this project again, I would want to conduct usability testing on more participants to gather more feedback on how to improve my website prototype.
